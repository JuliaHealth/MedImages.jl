\begin{abstract}
This report details the implementation, iterative development, and comprehensive benchmarking of Universal Differential Equation (UDE) models for voxel-level $^{177}$Lu-PSMA dosimetry. By embedding deep 3D residual networks within mechanistic pharmacokinetic equations, we demonstrate that SciML models can capture high-fidelity radiation transport physics ($r > 0.96$) while maintaining clinical real-time performance.
\end{abstract}

\section{The Mathematical Framework: Governing Equations}

The core of our approach is a 4-state UDE system integrated over a 300-hour (12.5-day) therapeutic window. The system explicitly separates biological kinetics from radiation transport.

\subsection{Activity Kinetics (Mechanistic PK)}
The temporal evolution of activity $\mathbf{A}(\mathbf{r}, t)$ follows a compartmental structure:
\begin{align}
    \frac{dA_{blood}}{dt} &= -(k_{10} + \lambda_{phys}) A_{blood} - \sum_{\text{voxels}} \text{Uptake} + \sum_{\text{voxels}} \text{Washout} \\
    \frac{dA_{free}(\mathbf{r}, t)}{dt} &= \text{Uptake}(\mathbf{r}, t) - (k_{out} + \lambda_{phys}) A_{free}(\mathbf{r}, t) \\
    \frac{dA_{bound}(\mathbf{r}, t)}{dt} &= k_3 A_{free} \left( 1 - \frac{A_{bound}}{B_{MAX}} \right) - (k_4 + \lambda_{phys}) A_{bound}(\mathbf{r}, t)
\end{align}
Where $\text{Uptake}(\mathbf{r}, t)$ is distributed spatially according to the initial SPECT activity $A_0$, acting as a proxy for local vascularization density.

\subsection{The Dose rate Equation (Universal Discovery)}
The absorbed dose $D(\mathbf{r}, t)$ is the integral of the dose rate $\dot{D}$, where we use a neural network $\mathcal{N}_\theta$ to discover the non-local transport residuals (cross-fire and heterogeneous scattering):
\begin{equation}
    \dot{D}_{total}(\mathbf{r}, t) = \text{Softplus} \left( \underbrace{\frac{A_{total}(\mathbf{r}, t) \cdot \lambda_{conv}}{m(\mathbf{r}) + \epsilon}}_{\text{Analytical Local Deposition}} + \underbrace{\mathcal{N}_\theta(\mathbf{A}_{std}, \rho_{std}, D_{approx, std})}_{\text{Learned Transport Residual}} \right)
\end{equation}
The use of \textbf{Softplus} ensures that energy deposition remains strictly non-negative, while the additive residual formulation allows the model to predict dose in regions where local activity is zero (capturing photon cross-fire).

\section{The Chronology of Development}

The final high-fidelity state was reached through four distinct phases of refinement, addressing critical bottlenecks in numerical stability and physical scaling.

\subsection{Phase 1: Multiplicative Physics (Initial UDE)}
*   \textbf{Strategy}: $\dot{D} = \dot{D}_{base} \cdot \exp(\mathcal{N}_\theta)$.
*   \textbf{Outcome}: Diverged.
*   \textbf{Insight}: Multiplicative residuals cannot predict dose in voxels where source activity is zero, making it impossible to learn cross-fire radiation.

\subsection{Phase 2: Black-Box Baseline (3D CNN)}
*   \textbf{Strategy}: Mapping SPECT/CT directly to Dose using standard 3D CNNs.
*   \textbf{Outcome}: Correlation $\rho \approx 0$.
*   \textbf{Insight}: Numerical ranges spanning 12 orders of magnitude (Bq to Gy) and lack of temporal constraints prevented the optimizer from establishing any spatial relationship.

\subsection{Phase 3: Input Standardization (UDE Breakthrough)}
*   \textbf{Strategy}: Implemented **Z-score Normalization** for all inputs and switched to **Additive Softplus** residuals.
*   \textbf{Outcome}: Successful convergence. Pearson $r$ reached **0.88**.
*   \textbf{Insight}: Standardizing features allowed the neural network to "see" tissue density gradients (HU) relative to activity peaks.

\subsection{Phase 4: Structural Stabilization (Final State)}
*   \textbf{Strategy}: Added **Group Normalization** (for batch size 1) and **Huber Loss**.
*   \textbf{Outcome}: Peak performance. Pearson $r$ reached **0.966**.
*   \textbf{Insight}: GroupNorm provided the intra-volume stability needed for 3D medical patches, while Huber loss neutralized the impact of noisy clinical SPECT pixels.
