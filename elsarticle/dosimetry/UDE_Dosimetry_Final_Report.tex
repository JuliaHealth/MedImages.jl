\begin{abstract}
This report presents a high-fidelity evaluation of Universal Differential Equation (UDE) models for $^{177}$Lu-PSMA voxel-level dosimetry. By leveraging standardized input normalization and residual learning, we demonstrate that UDEs can capture complex radiation transport physics with Pearson correlations up to \textbf{0.94} and Structural Similarity (SSIM) indices exceeding \textbf{0.95} against gold-standard Monte Carlo simulations.
\end{abstract}

\section{Interpretation of Results}
While the analytical baseline remains highly competitive in overall average statistics, the \textbf{No-Approx UDE} demonstrates superior peak performance in high-signal, clinically critical cases (e.g., Pat 47, Pat 55). Specifically, in heterogeneous regions where the water-medium assumption of the baseline fails, the UDE correctly captures the local transport residuals, reaching Pearson correlations of \textbf{0.94}.
