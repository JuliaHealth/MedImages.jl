📋 Executive Summary

The Lutetium-177 Prostate-Specific Membrane Antigen (177Lu-PSMA) Digital Twin utilizes Scientific Machine Learning (SciML) to bridge the gap between fast but inaccurate analytical dose calculation models and slow but highly accurate Monte Carlo (MC) physical simulations.

By partitioning the computational pipeline into Mechanistic Knowns (like classical radioactive decay, Hounsfield Unit density mappings, and unscattered ray-tracing) and Learned Uncertainties (such as tissue scatter, backscatter physics, and biological receptor saturation), we embed a neural network directly inside the governing physical equations. This ensures that the model remains physically bounded by thermodynamic laws while gaining the ability to learn and predict complex, patient-specific biological and physical anomalies.

📸 Stage 1: Single-Photon Emission Computed Tomography (SPECT) Initial Activity Calibration

$$ \underbrace{A_{true}(\mathbf{r}, 0)}_{\substack{\color{#f97316}{\text{Absolute Ground Truth (Bq)}}}} = \frac{ \overbrace{(A_{obs}(\mathbf{r}, 0) \cdot \text{Rescale Slope}) + \text{Rescale Intercept}}^{\substack{\color{#f97316}{\text{DICOM Float Conversion}} \\ \color{#f97316}{\downarrow}}} } { \underbrace{CF}_{\substack{\color{#f97316}{\uparrow} \\ \color{#f97316}{\text{Camera Calibration}}}} \cdot \underbrace{RC(\mathbf{r})}_{\substack{\color{#f97316}{\uparrow} \\ \color{#f97316}{\text{Recovery Coeff.}}}} } $$
SPECT Calibration: Converts raw scanner intensity counts from the Digital Imaging and Communications in Medicine (DICOM) image metadata into absolute physical radioactivity values in Becquerels (Bq) while correcting for detector sensitivity and spatial blurring.
⚠️
DICOM Double-Calibration Trap: If the SPECT DICOM image metadata Units Tag (0054,1001) already reads BQML (Bq/mL), the Camera Calibration Factor (CF) must default to \(1.0\). Failing to enforce this conditional bypass will cause a double-scaling error, leading to severe under-dosing in patient treatment plan simulations.

Constraint Scanner noise-correction algorithms sometimes accidentally output "negative" pixel values in empty space. Always filter these out using max(0, A).

🦴 Stage 2: Computed Tomography (CT) Anatomy & Physical Voxel Mass Normalization

$$ \begin{aligned} \underbrace{\rho(\mathbf{r})}_{\substack{\color{#0ea5e9}{\text{Mass Density}} \\ \color{#0ea5e9}{\text{(g/cm}^3\text{)}}}} &= \begin{cases} 1.0 + slope_{air} \cdot HU(\mathbf{r}) & \text{if } HU \le 0 \text{ (Air to Water)}\\ 1.0 + slope_{tissue} \cdot HU(\mathbf{r}) & \text{if } HU > 0 \text{ (Water to Bone)} \end{cases} \\[15pt] \underbrace{m(\mathbf{r})}_{\substack{\color{#0ea5e9}{\text{Target Voxel}} \\ \color{#0ea5e9}{\text{Mass (kg)}}}} &= V_{voxel} \cdot \rho(\mathbf{r}) \end{aligned} $$
Mass Mapping: Converts raw Computed Tomography (CT) X-ray attenuation values (Hounsfield Units, HU) into physical mass densities (g/cm³) and computes the exact physical mass of each individual 3D voxel.
⚠️
Computed Tomography (CT) Iodine Shielding Trap: High-Z contrast agents (\(HU > 300\)) trigger photoelectric attenuation rather than Compton scattering. If uncorrected, this triggers density calculation errors and AI mass hallucinations. The pipeline must filter out contrast-enhanced scans or clip attenuation bounds to safeguard AI density normalization.
Hounsfield Units (HU) Known Physics \(HU(\mathbf{r})\)
Definition 3D X-ray attenuation array evaluating patient macroscopic structural anatomy. Raw Computed Tomography (CT) values are typically stored as 12-bit integer values in the DICOM pixel data.
Constraint Bounded \([-1024, 3000]\). Subject to Iodine Contrast corrections (see safety alert above).

🔬 Stage 6: Physics-Informed Validation Tests

Before clinical deployment, the SciML model must be rigorously unit tested against physical tissue-equivalent phantoms (like the Catphan® modules) to ensure that the neural networks have successfully discovered correct physical invariants rather than unphysical noise correlations.

Test Scope Verifies the low-density density boundary check. Simulates a point source in a lung-to-air transition.
Assertion Asserts that density calculations clamp to \(\rho \ge 0.05 \text{ g/cm}^3\) to prevent division-by-zero occurrences and NaN outputs during Neural Network backpropagation.
Gradient Hallucination Check Known Physics CTP486 Module
Test Scope Tests spatial frequency noise. Feeds the AI high-resolution scans of uniform water phantoms to isolate noise from real borders.
Assertion Asserts that density gradient calculations \(\nabla\rho\) do not generate false boundary triggers (i.e., backscatter triggers remain inactive) in uniform environments.
Epoch Zero Identity Test Known Physics \(\mathcal{N}_\theta = 0\)
Test Scope Ensures baseline safety. Checks the output of the model when the neural network is initialized with zero weights.
Assertion Since \(\exp(0) = 1.0\), the output dose \(D_{phys}(\mathbf{r})\) must equal the classical analytical physical dose \(\frac{E_{base,total}}{m(\mathbf{r})}\). This ensures the AI behaves purely as a corrector.
